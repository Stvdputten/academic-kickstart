As you might have noticed, I skipped a week. It’s because I both had to focus on a specific part of my thesis and because there is a casus I had to put my mind to. My supervisor asked if I would be interested in applying for a PhD. At least he told me I would be capable of doing it. Well I’m absolutely stoked to do it, but I took a few days to think about it. My mind keeps switching between following a PhD or simply going into the industry. I’ll discuss my process so far and what I’m planning to improve my informed decision.

My first reaction to my supervisor asking me about my interest in the position: “Hell yeah, that sounds like lots of fun!”. I feel excited and I can’t think of anything else. I called my sister and basically we discussed for an hour, although mostly the negatives. If I’m planning to go to the industry anyways having a PhD is just a career suicide. Not only does it take around 4-6 years to get a PhD. The opportunity costs are probably the most of any PhD. To be an expert in the field of performance reliability of computer science sounds nice, but would I really be happy? So although it sounds great, I had to do more research before I can decide if it would be something for me.

Coincidentally on the same day a friend of mine and I got a drink. So I asked him about his opinion. Given that he’s from a completely different research branch (anthropology), he was very excited about such an offer. He said that it would be really amazing if I took it, because not everyone has such a choice. Furthermore I contacted a friend who’s in the industry, who basically replied: “Well it’s good to have known you”. Implying that it’s a complete suicide. The next day I contacted a study coach of my study, who told me about the pro’s and con’s. At that moment I realized that having a clear idea of how I want my life to be is very important. As such I can make the decision if going to the industry or academia makes more sense. It left me with the question, what would I need to be convinced of doing a PhD?

Confronted with this question I need to perform further steps. My career as an academia guy seems dubious at most. I don’t want to become a professor, but who really knows what your lives calling would be. I believe that growing as an individual is important and as such every path stays open and shouldn’t simply be waived away. I watched some videos about a PhD in the Netherlands and it seems nice enough. I can’t imagine myself being a student that does it a slacky way.

So what am I thinking right now? It seems to be a bad idea. I won’t earn a lot, while I need the money. Maybe in the far future. I don’t like the feeling of having a shortage of money to life myself, nor do I want to reduce my life expectancy by working in a branch that simply destroys your work-life balance. I still have a lot of assumptions about a PhD, but I need to apply to the industry sooner than later. I don’t want to be crawling back after a few years. I need to have my goals straight and know the costs of not going into the industry.

I’ll most likely make a decision within 1-2 weeks, most likely sooner.
